1Easy 5-Step Guide

Even beginners can create a relationship diagram in minutes.

1

Add a Node

Click "+ Add Node" to add a character. You can add a name and a short subtitle.

2

Set the Icon

Upload an image or choose an emoji or colored circle. You can also change the shape to circle, square, or bubble.

3

Connect with Lines

Hover over a node to see the + mark in the top right. Drag it to another node to create a connection.

4

Customize Relationships & Arrows

Click on a line to set relationship labels like Love/Hate/Rival, arrow style, color, and stamp decorations.

5

Export as Image

Click "⤓ PNG" or "⤓ JPEG" to download. Background patterns and titles are included in the export.

?FAQ

Is it really free?

Yes! This is a free web tool that runs entirely in your browser. No registration or installation required - just visit the URL and start creating.

Can I save my diagram as an image?

Yes. Click "⤓ Export ▾" in the toolbar, then choose "Save as PNG" or "Save as JPEG". The entire diagram including background patterns and labels will be exported as a single image.

Can I save my work and continue later?

Yes. Use the "Save Project" option to download your work as a .sankaku.json file. You can load it later to continue editing. All uploaded images are included. Note: This feature is available on desktop only.

Are uploaded images sent to a server?

No. All image processing happens in your browser. Uploaded images are not sent to any server - they're stored locally in your browser as an automatic backup. You can delete them using "Clear All".

How many nodes can I create?

Technically unlimited, but we recommend around 20 nodes for best readability and performance. More nodes may result in cluttered connections and slower dragging.

Can I use this on mobile?

Yes! We have a mobile editor optimized for touch. It features a bottom tab bar UI, guided flows for adding characters and relationships, and a screenshot mode. For precise editing, we recommend the desktop version.

Can I use the exported images commercially?

The exported images belong to you (the creator). However, please check the copyright status of any images or emojis you upload. See our Terms of Service for details.
